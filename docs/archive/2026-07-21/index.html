Technology is everywhere, but sometimes it fails us. Tell me about a time when you experienced a technology issue, such as an internet outage or a broken phone. What happened, and how did you handle it?

2AL 모범답변 + 전략 해설

🔒 스스로 답한 후 열어보세요 — 탭해서 열기

To be completely honest with you, I used to be someone who was constantly glued to the screen, you know? But last summer, I had this major wake-up call when my phone suddenly had a breakdown. It just went pitch black and wouldn't turn on at all.

At first, I immediately felt a sense of dread. I mean, we rely on our phones for literally everything nowadays—navigation, payments, and communication. Suddenly, I felt completely cut off from the world. It was like, "Oh my god, what am I supposed to do now?"

But, looking back, the thing is, it turned out to be an eye-opening experience. Since I couldn't use my phone, I had to basically go cold turkey on social media for three whole days until it got repaired.

At first, it was incredibly frustrating. But as I came to terms with my situation, I started noticing things around me. I actually read a physical book, talked to my family face-to-face, and enjoyed the quietness. It really hit me that I didn't need to be online 24/7. It was the perfect accidental break from screens.

Now, I try to consciously unplug. It really changed how I view my relationship with technology.

🧭 답변 뼈대
서론: To be completely honest with you~ (폰이 고장 났던 경험 소개) -> MP: At first, I immediately felt~ (당시 느꼈던 당혹감과 세상과의 단절감) -> 디테일 확장: But, looking back~ (폰 없이 3일간 강제로 소셜 미디어를 끊어야 했던 상황) -> 감정/생각: At first, it was~ (불안함에서 점차 여유를 찾고 깨달음을 얻은 과정) -> 마무리: Now, I try to~ (현재까지 이어지는 행동의 변화 및 교훈)

이 답변이 AL인 이유 3가지

✨ 구어체 필러인 'you know?', 'I mean', 'the thing is' 등을 문맥에 맞게 적재적소에 배치하여 자연스럽게 말을 이어가는 원어민스러운 흐름을 보여줍니다.
✨ 과거 시제(had, felt, couldn't, started)를 흔들림 없이 유지하면서 과거 행동과 깨달음을 효과적으로 서사했습니다.
✨ 감정을 나타내는 생생한 구어적 표현('felt a sense of dread', 'go cold turkey')을 사용해 상황의 극적인 면을 강조해 가산점을 유도합니다.
